S263 Photometry Lab

S263 Photometry Lab

This lab aims at teaching students how to plan and perform an observation run using an optical telescope and how to reduce data to analyze them.

Structure of the Lab

The photometry lab can be separated into the following parts:

  1. Oral Exam
    You need to pass the oral exam before you can continue with the lab. The questions are based on the topics and concepts discussed in part I of the lab manuscript. You must also have solved the tasks given in the lab manuscript. I strongly reccomend you to ask your tutor to briefly show you the telescope aleady before the exam, which will help you when preparing for the exam.

  2. Performing the Observation
    After you have passed the exam, you can schedule a night for the observation. You can chose the cluster to observe yourself or ask the tutor. If the weather is bad and no observations are possible, you will do a bad weather task. Data from a previous observation run will be provided to you to continue with the lab.

  3. Reducing the Data
    To analyze the observational data, we first need to perform a so called data reduction. Part II of the lab manuscript will give details on what steps you have to do for a succesfull data reduction. The reduction will be based on writing simple scripts with Python. In the appendix of the lab manuscript, you find an instruction on how to set everything up on your own computer. Alternatively, you can work from a computer at the institurte.

  4. Writing your report
    After you have managed to do the data reduction, you have 3 weeks time to write the report. In part II, you find also instructions on how to do the analysis. I strongly recommend you to send the report already a little bit earlier to the tutor and ask him/her for a quick read-through.
